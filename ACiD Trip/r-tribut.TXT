_:\  Nine god-damned years in the making, this colly.  I started it in '04  /:_
\:¯  as a nod to the artists that inspired me along my journey to Remorse,  ¯:/
 \   and then one day, I stopped working on it.  I kept telling myself I'd   /
 :\  get back to work on it "in a little while."  A little while turned     /:
 :/  into nine fucking years, and now, here we are.  A lot can happen to    \:
 :   you in the span of nine years; I'm now 31 years old; I'm paying off a   :
/:   mortage with my fiancee on a house out in the country; I'm a cubicle    :\
\:   rat, maintaining university web systems for a steady paycheck.  Life    :/
 \   is pretty good, if a bit simple by my hard-partying principals of       /
 :\  yester-year.  It is from this place that I return to my art.           /:
 :/                                                                         \:
 :   Looking back through the ASCII I drew when I was younger, I am--to      :
':   put it simply--ashamed of myself.  I am not ashamed of the art, but     :`
 :   of the brazen and ill-concieved commentary that all-too-often           :
 :   accompanied it.  I know that I was a teenager at the time (after all,   :
 :   I started with this adventure when I was only 12 years old), and that   :
 :   all teenagers do something they certainly wish they hadn't at some      :
 .   point or another, but it embarrasses me to no end.  Thank fuck for      .
 :   finally gaining some emotional maturity and perspective.                :
 :                                                                           :
 :   I'm glad that I'm in this new position of comfort and understanding,    :
 :   due to the nature of this particular colly.  This colly is an homage    :
 :   to the artists who most acutely influenced me and really blew my mind   :
 .   open with the true potential of ASCII artwork; well beyond that of      .
 :   the cheesy message board signatures and stick-figure doodles I was      :
 :   accustomed to seeing when adults would talk about "text art."           :
 .   Not every influence I've ever had is in here; far from it.  The names   .
 .   you see here are my early inspirations from Remorse, though I was       .
     also blown away by the style I found in many other groups of the era.

 .   I later came to know many of these people on bulletin boards (and       .
     later, IRC), and when the illusion (partially) dissolved, I found out
 .   how down to earth so many of them were, and how friendly and            .
 .   welcoming.  When I first decided that I was going to try my hand at     .
     ASCII, I was shamed into reclusiveness, and nearly lost all of my
 .   will to continue with the art form.  Thanks to both the friendly        .
 .   artists and the assholes alike, here I am today.  I have a style all    .
 :   my own, a renewed vigor for the art form, and a bittersweet nostalgia   :
 .   that I absolutely couldn't do without.                                  .
 :                                                                           :
 :.. These are my gifts to each of you.  I am proud to have made them.     ..:
